Welcome to the #bus Mental Health Support Chat

Need to talk? Come join us! We are a mental health support chat for those struggling with mental illness, the stress in their lives, or self harm. We are a community of people who listen and support each other.

The members of BUS are not professionals. If you are in suicidal crisis, please contact a crisis resource, your doctor, or local emergency services.

A Few Things First

We hope to maintain a safe and supportive environment in the chat. To that end, we do have some guidelines here. We ask everyone to read these before they join the chat.

We operate on a discord server. For legal reasons, you must be at least 13 years old to use the chatroom. If you are under 13, we encourage you to discuss your struggles with a teacher, doctor, parent, friend’s parent, or adult you trust.

We have members from all over the world and the activity level ebbs and flows over the course of the day. If it’s quiet when you join, we’d urge you to stick around for a bit or try again later.

Come chat with us in Discord.

About Us

#bus is a peer-support chatroom that was founded in 1996 as a safe, anonymous place for those struggling with self-injury or mental health issues to vent, seek advice, and feel a sense of community with others who understand. This site originated from another online community also named BUS, or Bodies Under Siege, which was a peer support resource for people who self harm. For many years, the online IRC chat room and the forum were operated together, but eventually the chat became its own entity. In time, the chat has evolved from strictly a self-harm support room to a place where anyone struggling with mental health issues can talk.

We consider ourselves to be a pro-recovery chatroom; however you do not have to be ready to make changes to join #bus, and many of our members aren’t. Within the “pro-recovery” mindset, we ask that you not actively discourage others from stopping unhealthy practices or seeking help.

The website and chatroom is run by volunteers.
